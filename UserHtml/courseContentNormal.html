Introduction to Visual Hierarchy

Visual hierarchy is the principle of arranging elements to show their order of importance. Designers structure visual characteristics so viewers can easily understand information.

Key Principles

  • Size: Larger elements attract more attention
  • Color: Bright colors stand out against muted backgrounds
  • Contrast: Dramatically different elements draw the eye
  • Alignment: Creates order and organization
  • Proximity: Groups related items together

Practical Applications

When designing interfaces, establish a clear focal point. Use size, color, and positioning to guide users through your content in a logical sequence.

For example, in a pricing table, you might make the recommended plan slightly larger and use an accent color for the "Sign Up" button to draw attention to the desired action.
